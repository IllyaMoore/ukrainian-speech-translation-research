Dear Ukrainians, briefly about this day. Energy — recovery efforts after the strikes are essentially ongoing continuously. This concerns not only Kyiv, but also Kharkiv and the region, Zaporizhzhia, the Dnipropetrovsk region, Dnipro, Kryvyi Rih, the Chernihiv region, the Sumy region — all regions where the situation is most difficult. Without question, Kyiv is currently facing the greatest challenges, and I expect the Kyiv authorities to intensify their efforts. Many cities and communities are ready to help. The government is ready to help. All instructions and resources are in place. One hundred and seventy-four repair crews are working. This is a significant resource that has now been directed to Kyiv. There are some results, but they are not sufficient. A significant number of buildings remain without heating, some of them for an extended period. I have instructed the government to allocate additional resources. The Ministry of Internal Affairs is working along its own line. We are scaling up support and warming centers. Everyone in the government will work with partners to obtain additional equipment as quickly as possible. I thank all our friends. These include various European countries — from Norway to Italy, Poland, Germany, the Baltic states. Many others who are currently supporting Ukraine, our cities, and doing a great deal for Kyiv. Separately today, I spoke with the Minister of Defense about the protection of energy infrastructure. I also spoke with the Commander of the Armed Forces of Ukraine — the command chain of the Air Force must be significantly faster in providing defense. Today there was also a full briefing from our delegation. The team returned after trilateral meetings. For the first time in a long while, there was again a format involving the Americans and the Russians. They managed to discuss various issues of a more military nature. Regarding steps toward ending the war and establishing real control and monitoring — there are issues that need to be prepared for the next meeting. Previously, we discussed that the teams would meet again on Sunday. It would be good if this meeting could be accelerated. Ukraine will be maximally prepared on all matters that need to be discussed and agreed upon. Ukraine always stands and will continue to stand on the side of peace, and the only one responsible for the continuation of this war is Russia. Real diplomatic results are needed so that there is no impression that the Russians are using the negotiation process with a deeply cynical and cruel purpose — pushing back new pressure measures against Russia that could actually work. And pressure is needed — precisely pressure, its consequences, the impact of sanctions, the blocking of Russian operations. All of this works toward stopping the war. Partners must not forget about this. I thank everyone who stands with Ukraine. I thank everyone who helps us. Glory to Ukraine!
