I wish you good health, dear Ukrainians. There are many tasks to attend to today, including for our diplomatic team. Ukrainian representatives report to me almost every hour. They are currently in the Emirates. Today, Ukrainian, American, and Russian delegations are there. An important conversation has already taken place — important because it has been a long time since there was a trilateral meeting format like this. They are discussing the parameters for ending the war. By now, they should have at least some of the answers from Russia. The key thing is that Russia must be prepared to end this war that it started in the first place. Ukraine's positions are clear. I have defined the framework for dialogue for our delegation; the delegation is in constant contact. Rustem Umerov, Kyrylo Budanov, Davyd Arakhamia, Serhii Kyslytsya. Tomorrow, General Hnatov, the Chief of the General Staff, and HUR representative Skibitskyi will also join the negotiations. As for the substance of today's negotiations, it is still too early to draw conclusions. We will see how the conversation goes tomorrow and what the results will be. It is essential that not only Ukraine desires to end this war and achieve full security, but that a similar desire somehow finally emerges in Russia as well. Today I received a briefing from Ukraine's Minister of Defence Fedorov. We discussed the situation on the front lines and our technological responses. I thank every unit that is defending our Ukrainian positions and significantly building up the technological component. Russian losses for December stand at thirty-five thousand. That is very serious. I also spoke with the Minister about personnel matters — not only within the Ministry but also across the branches of the armed forces, particularly the Air Force. It is a positive development that there is now more internal coordination within the Air Force and more thorough analysis of the circumstances of strikes. These conclusions are very much needed so that the system becomes more effective. I also instructed our team to work out, as swiftly and in as much detail as possible, our agreements with our partners regarding missiles for air defence. Yesterday there was a positive signal in the conversation with President Trump concerning Patriot PAC-3 missiles. Now we need a positive signal here in Ukraine — in the actual delivery to our defenders of the skies. The logistics must be accelerated to the maximum extent. I also held a special energy sector conference call. Every day begins in precisely this format — repair crews, emergency services, energy companies, and the State Emergency Service of Ukraine. Everyone is working around the clock, and there are results in restoration efforts, but overall the situation remains very difficult. This is especially true for Kyiv and the Kyiv region, the Dnipropetrovsk region, Dnipro, Kryvyi Rih, Kharkiv and the Kharkiv region, Zaporizhzhia, the Sumy region, and the Chernihiv region. We also reviewed the situation in Odesa separately.
