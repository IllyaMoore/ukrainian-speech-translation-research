I want to commend the work of the Ministry of Internal Affairs. I thank everyone involved — everything has been carried out with the utmost precision and focus regarding generator reserves and equipment, regarding real assistance to cities, especially Kyiv, and regarding heating points and support for the people. Unfortunately, many buildings in the capital are still without heating. The maximum number of repair crews and the maximum amount of equipment are being deployed to help people. I spoke with Prime Minister Yuliia Svyrydenko about programs that can support both the economy and local communities. As for generators and the ability for businesses to operate, the most accommodating programs possible are being put in place to help small businesses purchase and maintain generators. Government officials are to present all of this to the public today. There is also a decision to directly support each and every person on the repair crews. These are supplementary payments for every month of this emergency. We agreed on this today with the Prime Minister, as well as with the Minister of Energy — on the format of the payments and how people will receive them. Every worker involved in emergency restoration efforts will receive an additional payment of 20,000 hryvnias for January, February, and March of this year — 20,000 for each month. Further details will be provided by the government. And of course, I thank every country, all the leaders who are providing the necessary assistance. There are already, and there will be more, specifically energy-related aid packages — from the European Union, and separately from Italy, Germany, Lithuania, Britain, Poland, Japan, and America. Thank you to all of you who stand with us, who stand with Ukraine. Glory to Ukraine!
