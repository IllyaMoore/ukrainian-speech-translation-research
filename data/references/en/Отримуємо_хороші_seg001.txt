Dear Ukrainians, today has been an active day of diplomatic work. Today I spoke with Keir Starmer, the Prime Minister of Britain, and I also had conversations with the Prime Minister of Norway and the Chancellor of Austria. I want to express my gratitude for their support. There is very genuine interest in what is happening in the negotiations with the American and Russian sides. Our teams have been working actively today. Together with government officials and the Presidential Office team, we have been working on a document concerning reconstruction. I hope that our partners are equally committed to the tasks at hand. Clear security guarantees for Ukraine and all of Europe, absolutely clear commitments and opportunities for the reconstruction of Ukraine, and of course, real opportunities to bring this war to a dignified end. Right now, no one in the world can have any doubts. What kind of war is this? Who started it? Why is the war still going on? Everyone understands that this is Russia's war. And the responsibility to end the war is also Russia's. And pressure on Russia must be maintained. We are now receiving encouraging signals from Europe regarding the readiness of Europeans to apply more active pressure on the aggressor. It is very important that this pressure targets, in particular, Russia's tanker fleet and the entire infrastructure of Russian oil exports. It would greatly help all of us in Europe if Russian revenues were cut even further. We are already seeing enormous new problems for the aggressor with its federal budget, as well as with inflation and economic indicators. These are all good, right signals that peace may yet come. The harder it is for Russia, the closer peace becomes. With the Prime Minister of Britain, we spoke in particular about these strikes and about the need to protect our state and our people. Today in Odesa, throughout the day, rescue operations and debris clearing have been underway following a Russian strike. Fifty-two attack drones were aimed directly at the city, many targeting the center of Odesa, the area around Privoz market. There were no military targets there — none whatsoever. Virtually all the targets of the Russian Shaheds were ordinary residential buildings. Simple civilian infrastructure — the strike was brutal. Most of the drones were intercepted, but unfortunately not all. Three people were killed; my condolences to their families and loved ones. Nearly 50 people sought medical assistance. That is one strike on one city. For example, in recent days there was also a Russian strike on the energy infrastructure of Kharkiv, using MLRS — multiple launch rocket systems. The damage was significant; restoration will proceed as quickly as possible. I want to commend Ukraine's State Emergency Service, Ukrenergo, Kharkivoblenergo, and all the specialists working to stabilize the situation. Everyone who is working right now for the resilience of Ukraine is working to ensure that life itself prevails. I also want to thank everyone working in Kyiv and the Kyiv region, in Zaporizhzhia, the Dnipro region, Sumy region, Chernihiv region, in all oblasts, in all communities in the aftermath of the strikes. There was also a Russian strike on gas infrastructure in the western part of our country, but everywhere our assistance is present, everywhere there is work being done for life and for the people. I thank everyone who helps, everyone who stands by our side. I thank everyone who fights for Ukraine as if fighting for themselves. Glory to Ukraine!
