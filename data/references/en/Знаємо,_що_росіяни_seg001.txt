I wish you good health! A brief summary of today. Repair crews and the necessary forces for restoration have been deployed in all regions where there is a need. The most difficult situation right now is in Kyiv — first and foremost with heating. There are also significant problems with electricity. Crews from many of our regions have been brought in for Kyiv — effectively from across the entire country — and even front-line regions are ready to help. I also want to thank Ukrzaliznytsia, other state-owned companies, and everyone in the private sector who is supporting people. The Ministry of Internal Affairs of Ukraine provided hot meals in the districts of Kyiv where it was needed. In the past day alone — 6,200 people in Kyiv. In the Kyiv region, 22,000 people received hot meals. We are prepared to scale up this assistance if necessary. I expect reports on all those instructions regarding work with partners that were issued. The search for and procurement of equipment should have begun before this winter. The city authorities in Kyiv must act significantly faster now, so that at least by February things become easier for people — especially since everyone in the government is ready to help. Regular reports on the situation in Chernihiv, in the Sumy region, and in the Dnipropetrovsk region. In Kryvyi Rih, heat supply was being restored throughout the day. As of this morning, more than two hundred buildings had no heating following the Russian strike. Kharkiv region, Odesa region, Zaporizhzhia. I want to thank everyone who is working to ensure that people have heat and light. There are also significant outages in the Poltava region, as well as in the Kirovohrad region, the Mykolaiv region, and in the Vinnytsia and Chernivtsi regions. I spoke with the President of France. Emmanuel noted the challenges that exist. We discussed the assistance Ukraine needs most right now. We count on Europe's support. It is important that leaders and states do not remain silent about what is happening — that they do not remain silent about the fact that the Russians are still trying to inflict as much pain on Ukrainians as possible, regardless of any diplomacy. We also know that the Russians are preparing for a new strike. Intelligence is providing information on this. It is essential that right now America, right now Europe, right now all partners understand how every single Russian strike discredits diplomatic talks. Today, in the Kyiv region, the Russians killed a woman and a man. They were simply in their apartment. My condolences to their family and loved ones. A four-year-old child was saved. I want to thank Maryan Kushnir, a journalist with Radio Liberty, a war correspondent, who acted courageously and rescued that little girl. So much depends precisely on the fact that our people do not lose their courage.
