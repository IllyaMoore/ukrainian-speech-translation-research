Dear Ukrainians, today has been an active day of diplomatic work. Today I spoke with Keir Starmer, the Prime Minister of Britain, and today I also had conversations with the Prime Minister of Norway and the Chancellor of Austria. I want to thank them for their support. There is very sincere interest in what is happening in negotiations with America and the Russian side. Our teams are working actively today. Just now, we with government officials and the Presidential Office team were working on a document regarding reconstruction, and I hope that our partners have the same approach to these tasks. Clear security guarantees for Ukraine and all of Europe, absolutely clear commitments and opportunities for Ukraine's reconstruction, and, of course, real opportunities to end this war with dignity. Right now, no one in the world can have any doubts. What kind of war is this? Who started it? Why does the war continue? Everyone understands that this is Russia's war. And the responsibility is also Russia's to end the war. And there must be pressure on Russia. We are now receiving good signals from Europe regarding Europeans' readiness to pressure the aggressor more actively. It is very important that this be pressure, in particular, on Russia's tanker fleet, on all infrastructure of Russian oil exports. This will help all of us in Europe very much if Russian revenues are cut even more. We are already seeing colossal new problems for the aggressor with the federal budget, as well as with inflation and economic indicators. These are all good, correct signals for peace to finally come. The harder it is for Russia, the closer peace becomes. I spoke with the British Prime Minister, in particular, about these strikes, about the need to protect our state and people. Today in Odesa throughout the day there were rescue operations, clearing rubble after a Russian strike - 52 strike drones specifically against the city, many against the center of Odesa, the area around Pryvoz. There were no military targets there, there are none. And virtually all targets of the Russian Shaheds were ordinary buildings. Simple civilian infrastructure, it was a brutal strike. Most drones were successfully shot down, but unfortunately, not all. Three people died, my condolences to their families and loved ones. Almost 50 people sought help. This is one strike on one city. For example, today there was also a Russian strike on Kharkiv's energy infrastructure, a MLRS strike, multiple launch rocket systems. The damage was significant, restoration will be as fast as possible, as much as possible. I want to recognize our State Emergency Service of Ukraine and Ukrenergo, and Kharkiv Oblenergo, all specialists who are working to stabilize this situation. Everyone who is working now for Ukraine's stability is working so that life, as such, prevails. I also want to thank everyone who is working in Kyiv and Kyiv region, in Zaporizhzhia region, Dnipropetrovsk region, Sumy region, Chernihiv region, in all regions, in all communities after the strikes. There was also a Russian strike on gas infrastructure in the western part of our state, but everywhere there is our help, everywhere there is work for the sake of life and people. I thank everyone who helps, those who are nearby. I thank everyone who fights for Ukraine as for themselves. Glory to Ukraine!
