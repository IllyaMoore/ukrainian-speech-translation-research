# Distinguished Ukrainians,

Right now in Kharkiv and Dnipro and in our other cities and communities, the elimination of the consequences of Russian strikes is underway. In Kyiv today, the Russian army damaged another hospital, an absolutely civilian facility. In Dnipro, among the hits is a civilian enterprise, a food producer—sunflower oil, of course—and apparently this is also a target for Russia. In Kharkiv, the Russians struck directly at heat, at energy for people, at ordinary life, with ballistic weapons, against energy infrastructure. In Kherson, energy repair crews were working to restore electricity supply after Russian strikes. And all of this only testifies to the fact that Russia does not seriously perceive the diplomatic work that civilized countries are attempting to conduct with it. Russia, prolonging the war, is trying to inflict as much damage as possible on Ukraine. Their strategy is unchanged. Our strategy—the strategy of protecting life—will be strengthened. It is already doing so. First, yesterday and today I held meetings with employees of the Security Service of Ukraine. I am grateful to Vasyl Malyuk for strong combat work, for asymmetrical operations, which he knows how to conduct. Vasyl will continue to engage in such operations first and foremost. We discussed this work with him. The Security Service has complete political support for our asymmetrical actions against Russia. Yevhenii Khmar—an experienced person. He has just been appointed as acting head of the Security Service of Ukraine. Khmara heads the Center for Special Operations A of the SBU. I also spoke with Denys Kilymnik, first deputy commander of Alpha. He is one of those who delivers precisely those strikes against the enemy that the Russians fear and that add motivation to Ukrainians. I spoke with Vasyl Kozak, an SBU colonel—a young man, one of representatives of a new generation of Ukrainian defenders, SBU fighters, a master of operations. We discussed with them what has already been done and what we still plan to do. I support this work and am grateful for a fresh perspective on the SBU's operations. I spoke with Oleksandr Pokład. It is important that the internal protection of Ukrainian statehood and the counterintelligence component of the SBU's work have been significantly strengthened over the years of this war. The SBU's potential will be greater—precisely in the context of protecting Ukraine, protecting our positions, protecting national interests. I thank everyone who works. Personnel decisions will follow in this manner. Today there was also an important conversation with Mykhailo Fedorov. He presented the format of renewal at the Ministry of Defence of Ukraine—what the priorities should be, what decisions we need to make.
