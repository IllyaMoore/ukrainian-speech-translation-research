Dear Ukrainians, today has been an active day of diplomatic work. Today I spoke with Keir Starmer, the Prime Minister of Britain, and today I also had conversations with the Prime Minister of Norway and the Chancellor of Austria. I want to thank them for their support. I am very genuinely interested in what is happening in negotiations with America and the Russian side. Our teams are working actively today. We have just been working with the government officials and the team of the Presidential Office on a document regarding reconstruction, and I hope that our partners approach these tasks with the same seriousness. Clear security guarantees for Ukraine and all of Europe, absolutely clear commitments and opportunities for Ukraine's reconstruction, and, of course, real possibilities to end this war with dignity. There should be no doubt now for anyone in the world. What kind of war is this? Who started it? Why is the war still ongoing? Everyone understands that this is a Russian war. And the responsibility to end it is also Russia's. And there must be pressure on Russia. We are now receiving good signals from Europe regarding the readiness of Europeans to press more actively on the aggressor. It is very important that this be pressure, in particular, on Russia's tanker fleet, on all of Russia's export infrastructure. This will help all of us in Europe greatly if Russian revenues are cut even further. We are already seeing colossal new problems for the aggressor with the federal budget, as well as with inflation and economic indicators. These are all good, correct signals for peace to finally come. The more difficult it is for Russia, the closer peace is. I spoke with the Prime Minister of Britain, in particular, about these strikes, about the need to protect our state and people. Today in Odesa, throughout the day, rescue operations and debris clearing are underway following a Russian strike with 52 attack drones precisely against the city, many targeting the center of Odesa, the area around the port. There were no military facilities there, there are none. And essentially all the targets of Russian strikes are ordinary houses. Simple civilian infrastructure, it was a severe strike. Most of the drones were successfully shot down, but unfortunately not all. Three people were killed; my condolences to their families and loved ones. Almost 50 people sought assistance. This is just one strike on one city. For example, earlier today there was also a Russian strike on Kharkiv's energy infrastructure, a strike with multiple launch rocket systems. There was significant damage, restoration will be as rapid as possible. I want to note the work of our State Emergency Service of Ukraine and the energy companies of Kharkiv region, and all the specialists who are working to stabilize this situation. Everyone who is working now, for the sake of Ukraine's stability, is working so that life itself prevails. I also want to thank everyone who is working in Kyiv and the Kyiv region, in Zaporizhzhia, in the Dnipro region, Sumy region, Chernihiv region, in all regions, in all communities following the strikes. There was also a Russian strike on gas infrastructure in the western part of our state, but everywhere we have our assistance, everywhere there is work for the sake of life and people. I thank everyone who helps, those who stand beside us. I thank everyone who fights for Ukraine, as if for themselves. Glory to Ukraine!
