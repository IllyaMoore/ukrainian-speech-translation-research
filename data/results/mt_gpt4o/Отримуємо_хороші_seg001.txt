Dear Ukrainians, today is an active day of diplomatic work. Today I spoke with Keir Starmer, the Prime Minister of Britain, and also had conversations with the Prime Minister of Norway and the Chancellor of Austria. I want to thank them for their support. It is very interesting to see the negotiations with America and the Russian side. Our teams are working actively today. We, along with government officials and the President's Office team, worked on a document regarding reconstruction, and I hope our partners are equally committed to these tasks. Clear security guarantees for Ukraine and all of Europe, absolutely clear commitments and opportunities for the reconstruction of Ukraine, and, of course, real opportunities to end this war with dignity. There should be no doubt in anyone's mind around the world. What is this war about? Who started it? Why does the war still continue? Everyone understands that this is a Russian war. And the responsibility is also Russian to end the war. There must be pressure on Russia. We are now receiving good signals from Europe regarding the Europeans' readiness to exert more active pressure on the aggressor. It is very important that this pressure includes the Russian tanker fleet and the entire infrastructure of Russian oil exports. This will greatly help all of us in Europe if Russian revenues are further cut. We are already seeing colossal new problems for the aggressor with the federal budget, as well as with inflation and economic indicators. These are all good, correct signals for peace to finally come. The more difficult it is for Russia, the closer peace is. We spoke with the British Prime Minister, in particular, about these attacks, about the need to protect our state and people. Today in Odesa, throughout the day, rescue operations and debris clearance took place after a Russian attack with 52 strike drones specifically targeting the city, many against the center of Odesa, the area around the market. There were no military targets there, none. And virtually all the targets of the Russian Shaheds were ordinary buildings. Simple civilian infrastructure, it was a harsh blow. Most of the drones were shot down, but unfortunately not all. Three people died, my condolences to their families and loved ones. Nearly 50 people sought help. This is one strike on one city. For example, the day before there was another Russian strike on the energy infrastructure of Kharkiv, a strike with MLRS, multiple launch rocket systems. There was significant damage, and restoration will be as fast as possible. I want to commend our State Emergency Service of Ukraine and Ukrenergo, and all the specialists working to stabilize this situation. Everyone who is working now for the resilience of Ukraine is working so that life, as such, prevails. I also want to thank everyone working in Kyiv, in the Kyiv region, in Zaporizhzhia, in the Dnipro region, Sumy region, Chernihiv region, in all regions, in all communities after the strikes. There was also a Russian strike on the gas infrastructure in the western part of our country, but everywhere there is our help, everywhere there is work for the sake of life and people. I thank everyone who helps, those who are nearby. I thank everyone who fights for Ukraine as for themselves. Glory to Ukraine!
