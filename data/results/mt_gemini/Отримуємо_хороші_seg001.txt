Fellow Ukrainians, today is an active day of diplomatic work. Today I spoke with Keir Starmer, the Prime Minister of Britain, and today I also had conversations with the Prime Minister of Norway and the Chancellor of Austria. I want to thank them for their support. There is very sincere interest in the negotiations with America and the Russian side. Our teams are working actively today. We, together with government officials and the team of the Office of the President, have been working on a document regarding reconstruction, and I hope that our partners have the same approach to the tasks. Clear security guarantees for Ukraine and all of Europe, absolutely clear obligations and opportunities for the reconstruction of Ukraine, and, of course, real opportunities to end this war with dignity. Right now, no one in the world can have any doubts. What kind of war is this? Who started it? Why is the war still going on? Everyone understands that this is a Russian war. And Russia also has the responsibility to end the war. And there must be pressure on Russia. We are now receiving good signals from Europe regarding the willingness of Europeans to put more active pressure on the aggressor. It is very important that this pressure is exerted, in particular, on Russia's tanker fleet, on the entire infrastructure of Russian oil exports. This will greatly help all of us in Europe if Russian revenues are cut off even more. We are already seeing colossal new problems for the aggressor with the federal budget, as well as with inflation and economic indicators. These are all good, correct signals that peace will still come. The more difficult it is for Russia, the closer peace is. I spoke with the Prime Minister of Britain, in particular, about these strikes, about the need to protect our state and people. Today in Odesa, rescue operations are underway throughout the day, clearing the rubble after the Russian strike. 52 strike drones specifically against the city, many against the center of Odesa, the area around the Pryvoz market. There were no military facilities there, there are none. And in fact, all the targets of Russian Shaheds are ordinary houses. Simple civilian infrastructure, it was a harsh strike. Most of the drones were shot down, but, unfortunately, not all. Three people died, my condolences to their families and friends. Almost 50 people sought help. This is one strike on one city. For example, the day before there was another Russian strike on the energy infrastructure of Kharkiv, an MLRS strike, multiple launch rocket systems. There was significant damage, the restoration will be as quick as possible, as much as possible. I want to commend our State Emergency Service of Ukraine and Ukrenergo, and Kharkivoblenergo, all the specialists who are working to stabilize this situation. Everyone who is working now for the sake of Ukraine's resilience is working to ensure that life, as such, prevails. I also want to thank everyone who works in Kyiv and the Kyiv region, in Zaporizhzhia, in the Dnipro region, in the Sumy region, in the Chernihiv region, in all regions, in all communities after the strikes. There was also a Russian strike on the gas infrastructure in the western part of our state, but everywhere there is our help, everywhere there is work for the sake of life and people. I thank everyone who helps, those who are nearby. I thank everyone who fights for Ukraine, as for themselves. Glory to Ukraine!
